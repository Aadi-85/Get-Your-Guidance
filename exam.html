Joint Engineering Exam

  1. JEE Main
    • JEE Main is conducted by the National Testing Agency (NTA) in India.
    • It is the qualifying exam for admission to undergraduate engineering programs in prestigious institutions like the Indian Institutes of Technology (IITs), National Institutes of Technology (NITs), and other centrally funded technical institutions.
    • JEE Main is typically held twice a year, in January and April.

  2. JEE Advanced
    • JEE Advanced is the second level of examination and is conducted by one of the IITs (Indian Institutes of Technology) on a rotational basis.
    • Only candidates who have qualified in JEE Main are eligible to appear for JEE Advanced.
    • Successful candidates in JEE Advanced are eligible for admission to the IITs, which are among the premier engineering institutes in India.

  3. Eligibility Criteria
    • For JEE Main: Candidates who have completed or are appearing for the 10+2 examination or its equivalent with Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics are eligible to apply.
    • For JEE Advanced: Only the top 2.5 lakh candidates (based on their JEE Main performance) are eligible.

  4. Exam Pattern
    • JEE Main: It consists of two papers - Paper 1 (for B.E./B.Tech) and Paper 2 (for B.Arch/B.Planning). Both papers have objective-type questions.
    • JEE Advanced: It also consists of two papers with objective-type questions. The exam is known for its challenging nature.

  5. Syallabus
    • The syllabus for both JEE Main and JEE Advanced includes topics from Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics based on the 10+2 curriculum.

  6. Preparation
    • As the exams are highly competitive, candidates often undergo extensive coaching and self-study to prepare for JEE.

  7. Counselling and Admission
    • Admission to various institutes is based on the JEE Advanced rank, and counseling processes are conducted separately by different institutes.

National Defence Academy

  1. Purpose
    • The NDA exam is conducted to select candidates for admission into the National Defence Academy, which is a premier institution for training officers for the Indian Armed Forces, including the Army, Navy, and Air Force.

  2. Important Dates
    • UPSC releases notifications for the NDA exam typically in the months of January and June. The exam is usually conducted twice a year, in April and September.

  3. Eligibility Criteria
    • Nationality:- Candidates must be Indian citizens, subjects of Nepal, Bhutan, Tibetan refugees, or persons of Indian origin who have migrated from Pakistan, Burma, Sri Lanka, or East African countries with the intention of permanently settling in India.
      Age Limit: Candidates must be between 16.5 to 19.5 years old.

      Educational Qualifications: For Army Wing of NDA: 12th Class pass of the 10+2 pattern of School Education or equivalent examination. For Air Force and Naval Wings of NDA and for the 10+2 Cadet Entry Scheme at the Indian Naval Academy: 12th Class pass with Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics of the 10+2 pattern of School Education or equivalent examination.

  4. Exam Pattern
    • The NDA exam consists of two papers: Mathematics and General Ability Test (GAT).

      Mathematics: This paper is of 2.5 hours duration and consists of objective-type questions. It covers topics like Algebra, Matrices and Determinants, Trigonometry, Analytical Geometry of Two and Three Dimensions, Differential Calculus, Integral Calculus and Differential Equations, Vector Algebra, Statistics, and Probability.

      General Ability Test (GAT): This paper is also of 2.5 hours duration and comprises two parts - Part A (English) and Part B (General Knowledge). It includes subjects like Physics, Chemistry, General Science, History, Geography, Current Events, and English.

  5. Selection Process
    • Candidates who qualify the written exam are called for the Services Selection Board (SSB) interview. The SSB interview consists of two stages: Stage I and Stage II. Stage I includes Officer Intelligence Rating (OIR) tests and Picture Perception & Description Test (PP&DT). Candidates who qualify Stage I undergo Stage II, which includes Interview, Group Testing Officer Tasks, Psychology Tests, and the Conference.
      Candidates who clear both the written exam and SSB interview are eligible for final selection based on their overall performance and medical fitness.

  6. Preparation
    • Candidates should start their preparation well in advance and cover the entire syllabus thoroughly. Practicing previous years' question papers and mock tests can help in understanding the exam pattern and improving time management skills.

  7. Training and Career opputunities
    • Upon successful completion of training at the National Defence Academy, candidates are commissioned as officers in the respective branches of the Indian Armed Forces.
      The training at NDA is rigorous and covers academic, physical, and military subjects to groom candidates into future leaders of the Armed Forces.

National Eligibility cum Entance Test

  1. Purpose
    • NEET is conducted to facilitate admissions to undergraduate medical (MBBS), dental (BDS), and other allied health science courses in approved/recognized medical/dental colleges in India.

  2. Important Dates
    • NEET application forms are usually available online on the official NEET website.

      Candidates need to fill out the application form, pay the required fee, and upload scanned copies of necessary documents.

      Admit cards are issued to eligible candidates, which contain details about the exam center, date, and time

  3. Eligibility Criteria
    • Nationality: Indian Nationals, Non-Resident Indians (NRIs), Overseas Citizens of India (OCIs), Persons of Indian Origin (PIOs), and Foreign Nationals are eligible to appear for NEET.

      Age Limit: Candidates must be at least 17 years old at the time of admission or will complete that age on or before 31st December of the year of admission. The upper age limit is usually 25 years, with a relaxation of 5 years for SC/ST/OBC candidates.

      Educational Qualifications: Candidates must have passed 10+2 or equivalent with Physics, Chemistry, Biology/Biotechnology, and English as core subjects.

  4. Exam Pattern
    • NEET is a pen-and-paper based test (offline mode) consisting of one paper with 180 multiple-choice questions (MCQs).

      Subjects included in the exam are Physics, Chemistry, Botany, and Zoology.

      Each section consists of 45 questions, with each question carrying four marks.

      The duration of the exam is 3 hours.

  5. Application Process
    • NEET application forms are usually available online on the official NEET website. Candidates need to fill out the application form, pay the required fee, and upload scanned copies of necessary documents. Admit cards are issued to eligible candidates, which contain details about the exam center, date, and time.

  6. Preparation
    • As NEET covers a vast syllabus, candidates are advised to start their preparation early and follow a structured study plan.

      Studying from NCERT textbooks is essential as most questions are based on these books.

      Solving previous years' question papers and taking mock tests can help in understanding the exam pattern and improving time management skills..

  7. Career Opportunities
    • Qualifying NEET opens up opportunities for pursuing undergraduate medical and dental courses in reputed colleges across India.

      After completion of the course, candidates can choose to specialize in various fields of medicine or pursue further studies and research.

Common Law Admission Test (CLAT)

  1. Purpose
    • CLAT is conducted to select candidates for admission to various undergraduate and postgraduate law courses offered by NLUs and other participating institutes.

  2. Important Dates
    • CLAT exam is typically conducted once a year, usually in May.

      Notification regarding CLAT, application dates, exam date, and result declaration dates are released well in advance.

  3. Eligibility Criteria
    • Nationality: Indian Nationals, Non-Resident Indians (NRIs), Overseas Citizens of India (OCIs), Persons of Indian Origin (PIOs), and Foreign Nationals are eligible to appear for NEET.

      Age Limit: Candidates must be at least 17 years old at the time of admission or will complete that age on or before 31st December of the year of admission. The upper age limit is usually 25 years, with a relaxation of 5 years for SC/ST/OBC candidates.

      Educational Qualifications: Candidates must have passed 10+2 or equivalent with Physics, Chemistry, Biology/Biotechnology, and English as core subjects.

  4. Exam Pattern
    • CLAT UG Exam:-
      Duration: 2 hours
      Type of Questions: Multiple Choice Questions (MCQs)
      Subjects Covered: English including Comprehension, General Knowledge and Current Affairs, Elementary Mathematics (Numerical Ability), Legal Aptitude, and Logical Reasoning.
      Total Marks: 150 marks
      Negative Marking: There is a provision of negative marking where 0.25 marks are deducted for each incorrect answer.

      CLAT PG Exam:-
      Duration: 2 hours
      Type of Questions: Objective and Subjective type questions
      Subjects Covered: Constitutional Law, Jurisprudence, Administrative Law, Law of Contract, Torts, Family Law, Criminal Law, Property Law, Company Law, Public International Law, Tax Law, Environmental Law, and Labour & Industrial Law.

  5. Application Process
    • CLAT application forms are usually available online on the official CLAT website. Candidates need to fill out the application form, pay the required fee, and upload scanned copies of necessary documents.

      Admit cards are issued to eligible candidates, containing details about the exam center, date, and time

  6. Preparation
    • Candidates preparing for CLAT should familiarize themselves with the exam pattern and syllabus.

      Regular practice through mock tests and previous years' question papers can help improve speed and accuracy.

      Reading newspapers and staying updated with current affairs is crucial for the General Knowledge section.

      Candidates should also focus on improving their analytical and logical reasoning skills.

  7. Career Opportunities
    • Qualifying CLAT opens up opportunities for pursuing undergraduate or postgraduate law programs in reputed NLUs and other participating institutes.

      After completion of the course, candidates can explore various career paths such as practicing law, corporate law, litigation, legal consultancy, judicial services, academia, etc

The Birla Institute of Technology and Science Admission Test (BITSAT)

  1. Purpose
    • BITSAT is conducted to select candidates for admission to various undergraduate engineering programs offered by BITS Pilani, Goa, and Hyderabad campuses.

  2. Important Dates
    • BITSAT exam is typically conducted once a year, usually in May and June.

      Notification regarding BITSAT, application dates, exam date, and result declaration dates are released well in advance.

  3. Eligibility Criteria
    • For B.E. Programs: Candidates must have passed the 10+2 examination or its equivalent with Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics as core subjects.

      For B.Pharm. Programs: Candidates must have passed the 10+2 examination or its equivalent with Physics, Chemistry, and Biology/Mathematics as core subjects.

      Candidates must have obtained a minimum aggregate of 75% marks in Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics/Biology subjects in 10+2 examination, with at least 60% marks in each of the Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics/Biology subjects.

  4. Exam Pattern
    • Duration: 3 hours
      Type of Questions: Multiple Choice Questions (MCQs)
      Subjects Covered: Physics, Chemistry, Mathematics/Biology, Logical Reasoning, and English Proficiency.
      Total Marks: The total marks vary each year but usually range between 300 to 450 marks.
      Negative Marking: One mark is deducted for each incorrect answer..

  5. Application Process
    • BITSAT application forms are usually available online on the official BITSAT website.
      Candidates need to fill out the application form, pay the required fee, and select their preferred exam center and exam date.

      Admit cards are issued to eligible candidates, containing details about the exam center, date, and time.

  6. Preparation
    • Candidates preparing for BITSAT should familiarize themselves with the exam pattern and syllabus.

      Regular practice through mock tests and previous years' question papers can help improve speed and accuracy.

      Candidates should focus on strengthening their concepts in Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics/Biology, along with improving their logical reasoning and English proficiency skills..

  7. Career Opportunities
    • Qualifying BITSAT opens up opportunities for pursuing undergraduate engineering programs in reputed BITS campuses.

      After completion of the course, candidates can explore various career paths such as engineering, research, entrepreneurship, higher education, etc.
